# Cue Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 17, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how the Cue desktop app handles data.

## 1. Core privacy model

Cue is designed to process your work locally on your computer.

Cue does not require an account to use the desktop app.

## 2. What Cue processes

When you use Cue, the app may process:

- video files you choose
- extracted audio created during processing
- subtitle files and timing data
- style settings and project metadata
- exported output files

This processing happens locally as part of the app's normal functionality.

## 3. What Cue collects by default

By default, Cue does not collect telemetry or analytics and does not upload your media to a remote service as part of normal app use.

By default, diagnostics and support logs are disabled.

## 4. Optional diagnostics and logs

If you explicitly enable diagnostics or choose to share support information, Cue may save local diagnostic files or logs on your device so you can review or share them yourself.

Those files are not collected automatically by David Spivak.

## 5. Local storage

Cue may store local app data on your device, including:

- app settings
- project data
- subtitle and timing artifacts
- cached preview files
- optional diagnostics or logs, only when enabled or intentionally created by you

On Windows, Cue stores app data under `%LOCALAPPDATA%\\Cue`.

## 6. Sharing

Cue does not sell your personal data.

Cue does not automatically send your media files, exported videos, or local project data to anyone.

If you choose to email support, send diagnostics, or publish exported files yourself, that sharing is initiated by you.

## 7. Your choices

You can control whether optional diagnostics are enabled in the app by clicking the "Settings" heading seven times to reveal the diagnostics section.

You can also remove local app data by deleting Cue's local data folder and any exported files or project files you created.

## 8. Changes

This Privacy Policy may change in future releases of Cue.

The version published with a given release applies to that release.

## 9. Contact

If you have privacy questions about Cue, email davidspivak2@gmail.com.
