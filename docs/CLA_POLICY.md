# Cue Contributor Agreement Policy

How outside contributions are licensed and merged.

## Why Cue uses a CLA

Cue ships under `GPL-3.0-only`. The maintainer may want to relicense a future version. A CLA keeps that option open for code contributed by people outside the core team.

## Current policy

- Pull requests from non-maintainers require a signed contributor agreement before merge.
- Until a self-serve signing flow exists, the maintainer will send the agreement during review.
- If a contributor does not want to sign, the pull request will not be merged.

## What the agreement is meant to cover

The final agreement should let the maintainer:

- use, modify, and distribute the contribution as part of Cue
- sublicense or relicense the contribution in future versions of Cue
- continue shipping Cue under GPL for the current open-source line

The contributor should still be credited for their work.

## Scope

This policy page is a project rule, not the full legal agreement text.

Before accepting outside contributions for merge, Cue should have a real CLA document and a simple signing process.
